Build LaunchLens.

A market-intelligence agent that fuses what people search for with what actually sells, then tells a founder one thing: should you launch?

▶ Watch the 60-sec brief View the repo What to build ↓

The one-liner

A go / no-go copilot for founders.

A founder types a product idea. LaunchLens researches it live, then replies with a Go / No-Go / Niche verdict covering demand, price band and positioning, and keeps the conversation going with memory.

The core insight

Oxylabs tells you what is selling. SerpApi tells you what the market wants. Nobody connects the two. LaunchLens does.

Fixed brief, not a hackathon

Everyone builds the same product, so we grade engineering, not who had the cleverest idea. Your creativity goes into how well you build it.

The scenario

Founders are flying blind.

You are the founding engineer of a startup. Your founder walks in with this pitch:

"E-commerce sellers and founders are flying blind. Demand data lives on Google: what people search for, what is trending, what it costs across the web. Supply data lives on the marketplaces: what is actually selling on Amazon, at what price, with what complaints in the reviews. Nobody connects the two. We are building LaunchLens, an AI agent that fuses both and tells a founder whether a product is worth launching."

Your job: build LaunchLens from scratch as a standalone product.

In plain terms

Exactly what to build.

A command-line chat agent. If you can tick every box below, you have done the assignment.

A · Your agent must
  • Take a founder's question in a CLI chat loop
  • Pull demand from SerpApi (2+ engines)
  • Pull supply from Oxylabs (2+ sources)
  • Fuse both sides into one combined answer
  • Output a Go / No-Go / Niche verdict
  • Remember the chat and summarize long ones
B · Your graph must contain 45 marks
  • Graph + state — a typed StateGraph
  • Routing — conditional edges by intent
  • Fan-out — parallel nodes, then merge
  • Agent node + tools — SerpApi & Oxylabs
  • Short-term memory — checkpointer + summary

Wired together

Everything runs on one typed StateGraph, and the full state is saved to a checkpointer (SQLite or Postgres) after every node, so conversations survive restarts. A summarization node keeps long chats from blowing the context window.
